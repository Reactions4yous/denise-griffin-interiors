# Why this site converts — old vs new, and the funnel

## What was wrong with the old (Wix) site

1. **Wrong audience.** It marketed generic, online "e-design" to the whole
   internet — a minnow in the ocean, invisible against every influencer-designer
   on Earth. No local focus, so no local clients.
2. **Not her work.** The hero wasn't even her photo — a template image she
   hated, forced by Wix's limits (her real 6000×4000 photos would have been
   more than enough; the platform was the problem, not the photos).
3. **Confusing friction.** A sign-in area she never wanted — visitors don't
   know why it's there, and friction kills conversion.
4. **No conversion path.** No clear call-to-action, no booking, no local trust
   signals, weak SEO control. A dead-end brochure.
5. **Slow + templated.** Wix overhead and little control over speed or search.

## Why the new site converts better

1. **Right positioning.** Local, in-person, residential interior design at
   $100/hr. A small pond where she can be the biggest fish.
2. **Proof front and center.** Video hero of her own work, real portfolio, and
   a before/after that *shows* her skill. Proof builds trust; trust converts.
3. **Built to convert.** One clear CTA per section, a contact form + booking,
   and a "trust staircase": beautiful → who she is → proof → services → contact.
4. **Found in search + AI.** 8 local city pages, LocalBusiness + FAQ schema,
   answer-first copy — so she shows up for "interior designer Wesley Chapel"
   in Google *and* in ChatGPT/Google AI answers.
5. **Fast + secure + static** — no friction, scales under traffic, ranks well.

## The funnel — how the pieces fit together

Think of it as one machine: **Attract → Capture → Book → Nurture → Convert**,
fed by two traffic sources (Google local + Instagram organic).

- **Google Business Profile (GBP) — the front door, do this first.**
  ~32% of local ranking. When someone Googles "interior designer near me," the
  map pack (top 3 pins) gets the call. GBP + reviews drive the pins; the website
  is the trust anchor that makes them pick *her* pin. Highest-leverage piece for
  in-person local clients.
- **The website — the proof & conversion hub.** Every channel points here. It
  turns a curious clicker into a booked lead.
- **Calendly — removes friction at "book."** Self-booking a consult beats email
  tag. Fewer drop-offs = more calls on her calendar.
- **Resend — automation & reliability.** Form submissions reliably reach her
  inbox and can auto-reply ("thanks — I'll be in touch within 1–2 days") so no
  lead feels ignored. Can grow into a nurture sequence later.
- **ManyChat on Instagram (later, if she's active there).** Interior design is
  visual, so Instagram is her natural audience. ManyChat automates DMs — someone
  comments "PRICE" on a reel → auto-DM with her info + booking link — capturing
  the organic attention her photos generate and funneling it to the site.

### The loop (flywheel)

Instagram attention → ManyChat DM → website (proof) → Calendly (book) → Resend
(confirm/nurture) → consult → happy client → **review** → which strengthens both
GBP and the site → which pulls in the next client. In parallel: Google search →
GBP map pack → website → same booking flow.

The old site was a dead end. The new one is a complete local funnel: right
audience, real proof, frictionless booking, automated follow-up, and two traffic
sources both feeding one conversion path.

### Priority order

1. Google Business Profile (claim + optimize + ask for reviews) — biggest lever.
2. Wire Calendly + Resend (booking + reliable form follow-up) — already built in.
3. Launch the site on her domain.
4. Instagram content cadence, then ManyChat — only once she's posting regularly.
